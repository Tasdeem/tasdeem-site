THE PROBLEM

The data exists. The architecture behind it usually doesn’t.

Most organisations have sustainability software running. They have dashboards, emission figures, and supplier questionnaires. What they rarely have is the operational evidence that makes those numbers defensible when a real auditor, a procurement team, or a regulator looks underneath them.

  • ✕ Dashboards built on assumptions rather than verified data.
  • ✕ Emissions figures that do not survive a structural audit.
  • ✕ Policies that exist on paper but have no operational workflows behind them.
  • ✕ Claims that looked credible internally and collapsed under a 20-minute external review.
